## Phonotactics

The basic syllable structure is (C)V(C).

### Onset

The onset position is optional. When present, it may be filled by any consonant phoneme, including palatalised consonants, except /ŋ/ and /ŋʲ/.

### Nucleus

The nucleus is compulsory and consists of a single vowel. All vowel phonemes are permitted in this position: /i ɨ e a o u/. Diphthongs are not phonemic.

### Coda

The coda position is optional. When present, it may be filled by any non-palatalised consonant, with the exception of /j/, which is permitted in coda position. Palatalised consonants are excluded from codas. /j/ and /w/ are written as ⟨i⟩ and ⟨u⟩ in coda position. [ʒ] is written as ⟨rz⟩ in coda position.

## Grammar

### Pronouns

|    | 1   | 2   | 3   | NDEF |
|----|-----|-----|-----|------|
| SG | vi  | ti  | si  | ki   |
| PL | vią | tią | nią | ki   |

Nouns do not decline for plurality.

### Word Order

Clong uses SVO word order.

```text
gatų szaną   e   pingą
cat  see.PRS OBJ apple
"the cat see an apple"
```

"e" is an object marker. Note that pronouns stack onto the verb like a suffix.

```text
szanąvi     e   pingą
see.PRS.1SG OBJ apple
"I see an apple"
```

### Tense

Verbs decline for tense.

| Tense   | Suffix |
|---------|--------|
| Past    | į      |
| Present | ą      |
| Future  | ę      |

### Negation

"ny" is an negation marker.

```text
szanąviny       e   pingą
see.PRS.1SG.NEG OBJ apple
"I do not see an apple"
```

### Questions

"li" is an question marker.

```text
liszanąti     e   pingą?
Q.see.PRS.1SG OBJ apple
"do you see an apple?"
```

### Imperative

"da" is marks an imperative.

```text
daszaną     e   pingą!
IMP.see.PRS OBJ apple
"see the apple!"
```

### Adjectives

Adjectives come before nouns.

```text
gorz rosz pingą
big  red  apple
"big red apple"
```

### Adverbs

Adverbs come before verbs.

```text
gial siemąvi
fast run.PRS.1SG
"I quickly run"
```

### Demonstratives

There are 2 demonstratives. "dia" and "ria" are equivalent to "this" and "that".

```text
diapingą  i     riapingą
PRX.apple COORD DST.apple
"this apple and that apple"
```

### Possession

Adding "ų" to a pronoun or noun makes it genitive.

```text
pingąvių      i     pingągatųų
apple.1SG.GEN COORD apple.cat.GEN
"my apple and the cat's apple"
```

### Conjunctions

Here are some basic conjunctions.

```text
pingą i     bynię
apple COORD banana
"an apple and a banana"
```

```text
szanąvi     lių siemąti
see.PRS.1SG SIM run.PRS.2SG
"I see while you run"
```

```text
hianąvi     zsę siemąti
say.PRS.1SG SEQ run.PRS.2SG
"I talk after you run"
```

```text
niapąvi     pingą gor  fąsįti      si  diacziofna
eat.PRS.1SG apple CAUS put.PST.2SG 3SG DST.place.LOC
"I am eating the apple because you put it there (lit. on that place)"
```
