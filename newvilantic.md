### Phonotactics

Vilantic follows a (C)V syllable structure, where:

* C can be any consonant.
* V can be any vowel.

### Allophones

* [n] is pronounced [m] before [p b] and [ŋ] before [k g]
* [Vi] and [Vu] are realised as [Vɪ] and [Vʊ].
* [Vji] and [Vwu] are realised as [V.(j)i] and [V.(w)u].

## Grammar

### Pronouns

Here is a chart of Vilantic's pronouns.

Pronouns follow a format of "PNC", where P is person, N is number and C is case.

#### Person Chart

| 0 | 1 (1IN) | 1EX | 2 | 2FOR | 3 | 4 (3.OBV) | PRX | MED | DIS |
|---|---------|-----|---|------|---|-----------|-----|-----|-----|
| í | a       | á   | i | é    | u | y         | e   | o   | ø   |

The zeroth person is used for indefinite referents.

First person inclusive/exclusive is for whether it includes the listener.

Fourth person is the obviative third person.

Proximal, Medial and Distal are for distances that are near to far from the speaker.

#### Number Chart

| NULL | SG | DU | PAU | PL | COL | UMB | MASS |
|------|----|----|-----|----|-----|-----|------|
| w    | x  | ŕ  | l   | ł  | r   | ý   | j    |

Null is used for none of an object.

Umbral is used when there are an unspecified number of objects or the speaker does not want to reveal the number.

Collective is used for a group of objects.

Mass is used for uncountable or mass nouns.

#### Case Chart

Vilantic has 8 cases: Nominative, Accusative, Genitive, Dative, Locative, Vocative, Comitative and Instrumental:

| NOM | ACC | GEN | DAT | LOC | VOC | COM | INS |
|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| a   | i   | u   | e   | o   | é   | ø   | y   |

To make `3PAU.DISTR.ACC`, we must find the components of the pronoun.  
In this case, P is "u", N is "l" and C is "i".  
To make `3PAU.DISTR.ACC`, we combine them to make "uli".

### Declension of Nouns

Declining nouns is similar to making pronouns, except that the "person" part is replaced with the noun.

Examples:

```text
riqoni
apple.COL.ACC
"that group of apples"
```
